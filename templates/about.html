My story:


For as long as I can remember, I have been fascinated with how things work. This goes for mechanical things such as cars and airplanes to technical things like computers and video games. If something does anything, I want to know how. My first memory of this was during my childhood when my grandfather gave me his old Apple II computer. This also came with a book with example programs that the reader could use to create mini programs on their own. I was young but very excited. This book had the answers to my overall question: 'How does this work?

This was also the beginning of my fascination with computers and software. Being full of confidence, I found the biggest program in the book. It was huge! I remember that the program was somewhere in the range of 300 lines of code! Laughable, now that I know how big programs can get. But I pressed on and carefully typed out every line of code. When I was finished, I ran the program and got nothing but a screen full of errors. I tried to decipher the meaning of the errors but was at a loss, so I gave up. As I grew up, my fascination never wavered, and as technology got increasingly more advanced, I decided to do something about my fascination.

After years of avoiding college for a myriad of reasons, I made the concrete decision to get a degree in software development. I could not wait to dive deep into the topic and finally convert my curiosity and fascination into knowledge and application. I realized that I would need a lot of time to dedicate to this endeavor, so I made the decision to quit my full-time job in favor of a 'part-time' job delivering pizzas at a local pizzeria. I had a feeling this particular location had high traffic, which meant great tip potential. The feeling paid off, literally. Now I had, accompanying my will, the means to pursue my goal.

The college experience was mostly positive. The best part was learning about the topics I loved and getting many opportunities to create things using my newfound knowledge. Before I knew it, I had accomplished my goal and obtained my degree. That brings us to this website.

I began applying for jobs the day after I graduated. A week later I was rejected by all of the companies. Sad, but not unexpected. I did notice a trend in the most the applications though. They often ask for a website, I didn't have one. They also want to see a portfolio of some sort usually GitHub, I didn't have one, except for my college projects but I felt those were to basic and not worhty of showing off. So I decided to make this website from scratch as a way to kick off my portfolio and use it as a way to shpw the world the things I am working on.

Thank you for visiting my site, I can't wait to continue building my skills and sharing them with you. Feel free to reach out to me via the contact page or take a look at my work on the portfolio page.
